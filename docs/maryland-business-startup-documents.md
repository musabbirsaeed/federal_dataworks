# Maryland Business Startup Document Packet for Federal DataWorks

> **Purpose:** This packet converts the Maryland Business Express startup guidance into a practical set of working documents for opening a Maryland consulting company. It is prepared for a new consultancy named **Federal DataWorks** and assumes the intended entity is a Maryland domestic limited liability company (LLC) unless you choose otherwise.
>
> **Important:** This is a preparation packet, not legal, tax, insurance, or licensing advice. Use it to collect information and coordinate filings. Review final filings with an attorney, CPA, insurance professional, or Maryland agency representative before submission.

## Source summary

Maryland Business Express organizes startup registration into these major steps:

1. Form the business with the Maryland Department of Assessments & Taxation (SDAT) through Maryland Business Express or by mailing SDAT forms and fees.
2. Obtain a Federal Employer Identification Number (FEIN/EIN) from the IRS after receiving the Maryland SDAT identification number.
3. Apply for Maryland tax and insurance accounts when applicable, commonly through the Combined Registration Application (CRA) or Maryland Tax Connect.
4. Confirm required state and local licenses or permits.
5. Purchase appropriate business insurance.
6. Consider employee health insurance if the business has employees.

Primary references:

- Maryland Business Express — [Register Your Business in Maryland](https://businessexpress.maryland.gov/start/register-a-business-in-maryland)

## 7. Insurance planning worksheet

Maryland Business Express recommends business insurance and notes that required or appropriate insurance depends on business size, employee count, industry, and coverage needs. It highlights common coverage types such as commercial auto, property, business interruption, liability, workers’ compensation, home-based business coverage, and other specialty coverage.

### Insurance to evaluate for Federal DataWorks

| Coverage | Likely relevance | Notes / next step |
| --- | --- | --- |
| General liability | High | Common client and lease requirement. Obtain quotes. |
| Professional liability / errors and omissions | High | Important for consulting, data, technology, and advisory work. Obtain quotes. |
| Cyber liability | High | Important if handling client data, systems, credentials, or sensitive information. Obtain quotes. |
| Business personal property | Medium | Needed for laptops, equipment, and office property. |
| Business interruption | Medium | Consider if revenue depends on office or systems availability. |
| Workers’ compensation | Required for most businesses with employees | Confirm before hiring. |
| Commercial auto / hired and non-owned auto | Low to medium | Evaluate if using vehicles for business travel or rentals. |
| Home-based business endorsement | Medium if home-based | Standard homeowners insurance may not cover business losses. |
| Key person life insurance | Optional | Consider if the founder is essential to continuity. |

### Insurance checklist

- [ ] Speak with an insurance broker familiar with Maryland small businesses and technology consulting.
- [ ] Obtain quotes for general liability, professional liability, cyber liability, and business property coverage.
- [ ] Confirm workers’ compensation requirements before hiring any employee.
- [ ] Use the same legal name and addresses as SDAT, IRS, and Maryland tax registrations.
- [ ] Save certificates of insurance and policy documents.

This Operating Agreement is adopted by the sole member of Federal DataWorks LLC, a Maryland limited liability company (the “Company”).
